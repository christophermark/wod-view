Privacy Policy

The short version: WOD View collects no data. Your workout history lives only on your device. Nothing is uploaded, transmitted, tracked, or shared — with us or anyone else. There are no accounts, no servers, no analytics, and no ads.

What the app does with your data

WOD View is a viewer for your own workout history. You import a CSV export that SugarWOD emails to you, and the app parses and stores it in the app's private storage on your device. That imported data — workout titles, descriptions, dates, scores, and any personal notes the export contains — never leaves your phone.

WOD View has no backend and no account system. The app makes no network requests with your data. We (the developer) never see your workout history and have no way to access it.

What we collect

Nothing. WOD View contains no analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs, and does not collect usage data, identifiers, or diagnostics. The app's App Store privacy label is "Data Not Collected."

The one outbound link

The import instructions include a help button that opens SugarWOD's website in a standard in-app browser view, so you can find the export steps. That is an ordinary web page visit handled by iOS, subject to that site's own privacy practices; none of your workout data is included in it.

Deleting your data

Use Settings → Clear My Data in the app to remove your imported history from the device, or simply delete the app — all app data goes with it. Importing and deleting in WOD View never changes anything in your SugarWOD account.

Children

WOD View is not directed at children, and since it collects no data from anyone, it collects no data from children.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of the app changes any of the above (for example, by adding an optional online feature), this policy will be updated before that version ships, with the effective date revised.
